Unit 2: Literature Review and Research Proposal

In this unit I explored why literature reviews and proposal writing contribute to skill development at master’s level, while researching topics to cover for the aforementioned exercises. Leite et al. (2019) states that literature review and proposal writing play a crucial role in developing skills such as critical thinking, writing, language proficiency and enhancing information literacy. Furthermore, a well-crafted literature review is essential for developing a well-rounded thesis as it examines the main problem and anticipates hypotheses while maintaining audience interest (Leite,2019).

References

Leite, D.F., Padilha, M.A.S. and Cecatti, J.G. (2019) “Approaching literature review for academic purposes: The Literature Review Checklist,” Clinics, 74. doi:10.6061/clinics/2019/e1403.

Unit 7: Inferential Statistics and Hypothesis Testing

Inferential statistics is a key skill for data science and data analysis. It allows researchers to draw conclusions about large population using a sample (Anderect et al. 2011). In this unit I examined different inferential statistical methods on a various datasets to draw conclusions regarding the population set. This unit further explained the different levels of measurement which was explored in the Deciphering Data module.

References

Andereck, K. L. (2011). Inferential analysis of data.. Research Methods for Leisure, Recreation and Tourism, 213-225. https://doi.org/10.1079/9781845938918.0213

The unit also investigates the application of data visualisation tools to extract deeper meaning from statistical data.

Unit 9: Validity and Generalisability in Research

Unit 9 explores how the concept of validity and generalizability and reliability affect research design methods. Bashir et al. (2008) argue that while validity and reliability are crucial in quantitative research, their application in qualitative research is complex due to the subjective nature of human experience. To enhance research quality, there is a need to adapt qualitative research methods since validity and reliability are rooted in quantitative methods.

References

Bashir, M., Afzal, M.T. and Azeem, M., 2008. Reliability and validity of qualitative and operational research paradigm. Pakistan journal of statistics and operation research, pp.35-45.
